‚öî What is the Iron Harvest? ‚ñº

The Iron Harvest is a gothic medieval battle simulation played out day by day in a cursed arena of your choosing. Warriors from rival keeps are thrown together with no allies, no quarter, and no guarantee of mercy ‚Äî from each other or from the Gauntlet itself.

You are the lord presiding over this spectacle. You choose the arena, summon the warriors, name them if you wish, and then watch the chronicle unfold. Each day, every living warrior experiences an Action or Consequence drawn from a pool of hundreds of events ‚Äî and some meet their end entirely.

At the close of each day, an AI scribe composes a two-sentence gothic summary of everything that transpired and adds it to the chronicle. The game ends when a single champion remains standing ‚Äî or when enough time has passed that the lord grants a pardon to the survivors.

The Iron Harvest is a spectator experience. There are no decisions to make once the horn sounds. Sit back, read the chronicle, and let fate ‚Äî and a healthy dose of chaos ‚Äî do the rest.
‚ú¶ Setting Up the Harvest ‚ñº

Before sounding the horn, the lord makes two choices and optionally names their warriors.

Warrior Count
Choose 4, 6, 8, 12, 16, or 24 warriors. More warriors means longer games and more dramatic chronicles.
The Arena
Seven cursed grounds to choose from. The arena flavours the AI scribe's daily summaries but does not affect the underlying odds.
Warrior Names
Leave any card blank and a default name is assigned. Only custom-named warriors are recorded in the Chronicle of Victories.
Class & Keep
Each warrior is randomly assigned a class and keep at setup. These are cosmetic ‚Äî they appear in events and the ledger but carry no mechanical bonuses.

Each warrior card shows a number (their position), a class badge in ember, and their keep in dim gold at the bottom. Type a name into the field to claim them ‚Äî or leave it empty to let the Gauntlet choose.

‚òÄ How a Day Unfolds ‚ñº

Each day follows the same sequence. The battle overlay rises, the Gauntlet runs its calculations in the background, and the chronicle block fades in once the scribe has composed the day's summary.

Step 1 ‚Äî Deaths are resolved first. Before anyone acts, the Gauntlet decides who dies. Deaths are forbidden on Day 1 and Day 2 ‚Äî the opening days are for establishing the chronicle.
Step 2 ‚Äî Events are assigned. Every surviving warrior (and every warrior who died this day) receives one event. The dead receive their death narrative. The living each get either an Action or a Consequence, chosen at random.
Step 3 ‚Äî The scribe writes. The day's events, deaths, arena, and surviving warriors are used to compose a two-sentence gothic summary while the battle overlay plays. When hosted inside Claude.ai, an AI model writes this live. When hosted elsewhere, a local chronicle generator produces an atmospheric summary from the same data ‚Äî no internet connection required.
Step 4 ‚Äî The chronicle block appears. Previous days collapse automatically so the new day is always front and centre. You can re-expand any day by clicking its header.

Once a day completes, the ‚ñ∂ Next Day button appears at the bottom. Click it whenever you're ready ‚Äî the Harvest does not advance on its own.

‚ò† Death & Survival Odds ‚ñº

Death in the Iron Harvest is probabilistic ‚Äî nobody is safe, but nobody is certain to fall either. The Gauntlet evaluates each warrior independently each day. The probability of death depends on how many warriors remain.

Situation Death Chance Per Warrior Notes
Day 1 & Day 2 0% No deaths. The chronicle begins.
Day 3+ ‚Äî 4 or more warriors alive 12% Standard rate.
Day 3+ ‚Äî 3 or fewer warriors alive 25% Pressure increases as the field narrows.
The first day of a 4-warrior game 12% Small games use the standard rate throughout.
The last warrior standing is always protected. The Gauntlet will never kill the final survivor ‚Äî at least one warrior will always walk out of each day alive.

Death chances are evaluated per warrior, per day, independently. It is entirely possible ‚Äî and not rare ‚Äî for nobody to die on a given day, or for several warriors to fall at once. The Gauntlet is indifferent.

‚úß Events ‚Äî Actions, Consequences & Deaths ‚ñº

Each living warrior has a 60% chance of receiving an Action and a 40% chance of receiving a Consequence on any given day. The distinction is purely narrative ‚Äî neither is mechanically better or worse.

The event pools blend genuine gothic survival grit with physical comedy. A warrior might set an elaborate tripwire alarm, then accidentally walk through it themselves the very next day. The Harvest does not discriminate between the heroic and the hapless.
‚öú Victory Conditions ‚ñº

The Iron Harvest ends in one of three ways:

Last Warrior Standing. All other warriors have perished. The survivor is declared Champion and their name is etched into the Chronicle of Victories ‚Äî if they were custom-named.
Survival Victory (Lord's Pardon). If enough days pass with multiple warriors still alive, the lord grants a pardon. The surviving warriors share the victory. The day threshold scales with the number of warriors you began with.
All Fallen. Every warrior has perished. The Harvest claims all ‚Äî an ill omen even the lord has never witnessed before. There is no Champion.

üìú Chronicle of Victories & the Ledger ‚ñº

Wins are tracked across sessions in your browser's local storage. Every time a custom-named warrior wins a game, their victory count increments and their most recent class and keep are recorded.

Custom Names Only
Warriors using default Gauntlet names are never recorded. Only warriors you have personally named can join the ledger.
Shared Victories
In a Survival Victory, all surviving warriors are recorded as champions ‚Äî not just one.

‚öô The Game Master Panel ‚ñº

The Game Master Panel appears on the right side of the screen once Day 1 begins. It gives the presiding lord a set of tools to intervene in the Harvest.

Warrior List
Shows every warrior, colour-coded by status. Gold names are alive; red names have fallen.
‚úß Revive
The ‚úß button appears beside fallen warriors. Click it to return them to life ‚Äî the Gauntlet has no objection.
‚Ü∫ Start Over
Reloads the page and returns to setup. All chronicle progress for the current game is lost.

The panel is collapsible ‚Äî click its header to tuck it away. It remembers nothing between sessions; it reflects only the current game state.

The Navigation Panel on the left is always visible. Use it to reach the Ledger, this Guide, or to log out. It remains accessible throughout setup and during a game.
üïØ Tips for the Lord ‚ñº

Name your warriors after real people. The events read very differently when the hapless warrior who gets outwitted by a dungeon rat is your colleague.

Start with 8 warriors. It's the default for a reason ‚Äî long enough for the chronicle to develop personality, short enough that it concludes in a reasonable session.

Use the revive button sparingly. The Gauntlet earned that kill. Reviving a fallen warrior cheapens the drama ‚Äî but sometimes the story demands it.

Read the day summaries aloud. The AI scribe writes for an audience. The gothic tone lands better spoken.

Download your ledger occasionally. Browser storage can be cleared. If you have a warrior with a proud win record, back them up.

Large games (16‚Äì24 warriors) become a saga. With 24 warriors the pardon doesn't come until Day 60, and the pool of 139 actions and 141 consequences means almost no event will repeat before the game ends.
